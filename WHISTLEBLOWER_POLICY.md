## 1. POLICY OVERVIEW

This Whistleblower and Ethics Hotline Policy establishes SmartInvest's commitment to maintaining a workplace where employees can safely and confidentially report suspected misconduct, unethical behavior, and regulatory violations without fear of retaliation.

### 1.1 Purpose
- Provide safe reporting channels for misconduct
- Encourage early identification of problems
- Protect reporters from retaliation
- Maintain confidentiality and anonymity
- Ensure thorough and fair investigations
- Support regulatory compliance
- Foster ethical culture

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All employees and contractors
- Board members
- Consultants and advisors
- Third-party partners and vendors
- Anyone reporting conduct by SmartInvest personnel
- Suppliers and customers (may report)

### 1.3 Core Principles
- **No Retaliation:** Absolute protection against adverse action
- **Confidentiality:** Strict protection of reporter identity
- **Anonymity Option:** Report without identification
- **Good Faith:** Honest reporting protect even if findings differ from allegations
- **Prompt Investigation:** Swift and professional review
- **Transparency:** Regular communication with reporter
- **Regular Review:** Oversight by Board Audit Committee

## 2. WHAT CAN BE REPORTED

### 2.1 Reportable Conduct

**Legal & Regulatory Violations:**
- Criminal activity or fraud
- Money laundering or financial crimes
- Bribery or corruption
- Insider trading or securities violations
- Environmental crimes or violations
- Tax evasion or improper financial reporting
- Sanctions or embargoes violations
- Regulatory compliance failures

**Company Policy Violations:**
- Code of Conduct breaches
- Conflict of interest violations
- Discrimination or harassment
- Safety violations or hazards
- Data protection breaches or privacy violations
- Theft or embezzlement
- Falsification of records
- Unauthorized transactions or expenditures

**Ethical Concerns:**
- Questionable or suspicious practices
- Pressure to engage in unethical behavior
- Workplace bullying or intimidation
- Sexual harassment or abuse
- Unequal treatment or bias
- Concealment of wrongdoing
- Retaliation against other reporters

**Safety & Wellbeing:**
- Unsafe working conditions
- Health and safety hazards
- Environmental contamination
- Threats or violence
- Substance abuse at work
- Discrimination or prejudice

## 4. REPORTER PROTECTIONS

### 4.1 Anti-Retaliation Protections
SmartInvest prohibits retaliation including:
- Termination or layoff
- Demotion or reduction in rank
- Salary reduction or withholding of raise
- Negative performance evaluation
- Undesirable assignment or transfer
- Exclusion from meetings or information
- Harassment or intimidation
- Isolation from team
- Reduced benefits or privileges
- Threat of any of above

**Applies To:** All who report in good faith, even if allegations unsubstantiated (unless fraudulent report)

### 4.2 Confidentiality Protections
Reporter identity protected:
- Limited disclosure (need-to-know only)
- Not revealed to accused (if possible)
- Not disclosed to media or public
- Compartmentalized access to information
- Investigation handled with discretion
- Records maintained securely
- Data destruction upon case closure (per law)

### 4.3 Legal Protections
Reporters may:
- Disclose and discuss investigation (as needed for defense)
- Consult legal counsel
- Request legal aid from company (at company discretion)
- Receive legal support during investigation
- File external complaints (not breach of confidentiality)
- Refuse to participate in retaliation

### 4.4 Support Resources
Available to reporters:
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP)
- Counseling and mental health support
- Legal consultation
- Financial guidance
- Work schedule flexibility
- Relocation assistance (if needed)
- Anonymous monitoring of retaliation indicators

## 5. INVESTIGATION PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Initial Assessment (24-48 hours)

Upon receipt of report:
1. **Acknowledge receipt** (within 24 hours)
2. **Preliminarily assess** severity and credibility
3. **Determine confidentiality** level
4. **Identify potential conflicts** of interest
5. **Preserve evidence** and data
6. **Notify leadership** (as appropriate)
7. **Assign investigator** (independent party)
8. **Plan investigation** scope and timeline

### 5.2 Investigation Procedures

**Information Gathering:**
- Review relevant documents and records
- Preserve evidence (emails, files, communications)
- Interview reporter (detailed account)
- Interview witnesses (if applicable)
- Interview subject of complaint (if not investigating officer)
- Gather corroborating evidence
- Consult subject matter experts

**Investigation Standards:**
- Professional and objective approach
- Based on evidence, not speculation
- Documented with supporting materials
- All participants interviewed separately
- Confidentiality maintained throughout
- No prejudgment of outcome
- Opportunity for accused to respond

**Timeline:**
- Preliminary assessment: 2-5 days
- Investigation (standard): 10-20 business days
- Investigation (complex): 30-60 business days
- Interviews: Within 5-10 business days typically
- Findings: Within 30-60 days typically
- Urgent matters: Accelerated timeline

### 5.3 Findings & Outcomes

Possible outcomes:
1. **Substantiated** - Violation found and confirmed
2. **Partially Substantiated** - Some allegations confirmed
3. **Unsubstantiated** - Insufficient evidence to confirm
4. **Inconclusive** - Evidence conflicting or insufficient
5. **Withdrawn** - Reporter withdraws before completion

### 5.4 Reporter Notification
Reporter informed of:
- Investigation completion (general timeline)
- Final outcome (specific allegations or general outcome)
- Disciplinary action (if any)
- Any corrective actions implemented
- Appeal opportunity (if applicable)
- Remediation measures
- No ongoing retaliation concern

## 6. DISCIPLINARY ACTION

### 6.1 Potential Consequences
Substantiated violations may result in:

**Warning/Counseling:**
- Minor violations or first offense
- Written warning or coaching
- Training requirements
- Performance improvement plan
- Documentation in personnel file

**Suspension:**
- Serious violation
- 1-30 days without pay
- Condition for reinstatement
- Final warning
- Loss of bonus/benefits

**Termination:**
- Severe violations (bribery, fraud, violence)
- Repeated violations
- Pattern of misconduct
- Immediate dismissal (in egregious cases)
- Final paycheck per law
- Criminal referral (if applicable)

**Recovery & Remediation:**
- Repayment of improper gains
- Restitution to victims
- Correction of records
- Restoration of systems (if damage occurred)
- Civil liability (separate from termination)

### 6.2 Disciplinary Process
- Investigation completed first
- Findings reviewed by HR and compliance
- Accused employee notified
- Opportunity to respond before discipline
- Discipline determined by management level
- Documentation in personnel file
- Appeal process available (10 days)
- External verification (if appealed)

## 7. FALSE & MALICIOUS REPORTS

### 7.1 Good Faith Standard
Protection applies when:
- Report made honestly and factually
- Reasonable basis for report
- Not knowingly false allegations
- Reported in proper channels
- Cooperated with investigation
- Even if investigation inconclusive

### 7.2 False Reports
No protection when:
- Reporter knowingly filed false charges
- Allegations made with reckless disregard for truth
- Report made with malicious intent
- Reporter fabricated evidence
- Report made with intent to harm

**Consequences for False Reports:**
- May result in discipline (warning to termination)
- No legal liability for good-faith error
- Presumption of good faith unless clear evidence otherwise

## 8. CONFLICT OF INTEREST MANAGEMENT

### 8.1 Investigation Conflicts
Investigators must disclose conflicts:
- Prior disagreements with reporter or accused
- Financial interest in outcome
- Personal relationships involved
- Supervisory relationship
- Prior involvement in related matter
- Any other bias or prejudice

### 8.2 Conflict Resolution
- Independent replacement investigator assigned
- Oversight by uninvolved party
- Potential external investigator engagement
- Documented conflict and mitigation
- Reporter/accused notified of conflict management

## 10. CONFIDENTIALITY & RECORDS MANAGEMENT

### 10.1 Information Classification
Treated as confidential:
- Reporter identity and contact information
- Investigation details and findings
- Witness statements and evidence
- Internal deliberations
- Communications with reporter
- Any interim or draft findings
- Disciplinary deliberations

### 10.2 Access Controls
Information accessible only to:
- Assigned investigator
- HR/Compliance personnel (need-to-know)
- Executive leadership (summary only)
- Board Audit Committee (summary only)
- Legal counsel (if required)
- External auditors (limited)
- Court order or subpoena

### 10.3 Record Retention
Records maintained for:
- Active matters: Lifetime + 3 years
- Resolved matters: 3+ years minimum
- Substantiated violations: 7+ years
- Regulatory requirement: Per applicable law
- Regular destruction: After retention period

### 10.4 Encryption & Security
- Electronic records password protected
- Encrypted storage (at rest and in transit)
- Physical files locked and restricted
- Backup copies secured
- Backup destruction within 1 year
- Access monitoring and logging

## 11. SPECIAL INVESTIGATOR

### 11.1 Independent Investigator
For serious or systemic matters:
- Engagement of external investigator
- Legal independence from company
- Professional credentials and experience
- Conflict of interest assessment
- Full investigative authority
- Direct reporting to Audit Committee
- Final report to full Board

### 11.2 When to Engage
External investigator for:
- Board member involvement
- Executive officer allegations
- Criminal conduct (potential)
- Systemic violations affecting multiple people
- Matters requiring legal expertise
- Need for independence/credibility
- External confidentiality requirement
