## 1. FRAMEWORK OVERVIEW

This Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) Framework establishes SmartInvest's comprehensive approach to identifying, analyzing, evaluating, monitoring, and mitigating risks across all business operations.

### 1.1 Purpose
- Systematically identify and assess risks
- Support strategic decision-making
- Reduce unplanned losses and surprises
- Optimize resource allocation
- Ensure regulatory compliance
- Protect stakeholder value
- Foster risk-aware culture

### 1.2 Scope
Applies to:
- All business units and departments
- All levels of the organization
- All operational processes
- Strategic initiatives and projects
- Subsidiary companies
- Third-party providers
- Board and executive management

### 1.3 Risk Management Principles
- **Holistic:** Considers all risk categories
- **Systematic:** Follows defined processes
- **Documented:** Evidence-based decisions
- **Continuous:** Ongoing monitoring and improvement
- **Integrated:** Linked to strategy execution
- **Communication:** Transparent and frequent
- **Accountability:** Clear ownership

## 4. RISK ASSESSMENT PROCESS

### 4.1 Risk Identification
Risks identified through:
- Regular brainstorming sessions (quarterly)
- Business unit self-assessments (annual)
- Incident and loss analysis
- Industry and peer benchmarking
- Regulatory change monitoring
- Customer and supplier feedback
- Process mapping and review
- Emerging risks monitoring

### 4.2 Risk Analysis
For each identified risk:

**Likelihood Assessment:**
- Almost Certain (80-100%)
- Likely (60-80%)
- Possible (40-60%)
- Unlikely (20-40%)
- Rare (0-20%)

**Impact Assessment:**
- Catastrophic: > KES 50 million or existential threat
- Major: KES 10-50 million or significant business impact
- Moderate: KES 1-10 million or notable impact
- Minor: < KES 1 million or limited impact
- Negligible: Immaterial impact

### 4.3 Risk Rating
| Likelihood | Catastrophic | Major | Moderate | Minor | Negligible |
|-----------|-------------|-------|----------|-------|-----------|
| **Almost Certain** | Critical | High | High | Medium | Low |
| **Likely** | High | High | Medium | Medium | Low |
| **Possible** | High | Medium | Medium | Low | Low |
| **Unlikely** | Medium | Medium | Low | Low | Negligible |
| **Rare** | Medium | Low | Low | Negligible | Negligible |

**Risk Scoring:** Likelihood Ã— Impact = Risk Score

## 6. RISK MITIGATION STRATEGIES

### 6.1 Four Response Options

**Avoid:** Eliminate the risk by not pursuing activity
- When: Risk outweighs potential benefit
- Example: Exiting risky market or product line
- Cost: Lost opportunities
- Effectiveness: Highest (eliminates risk)

**Mitigate:** Reduce likelihood or impact
- When: Risk manageable through controls
- Example: Cybersecurity investments
- Cost: Control implementation investment
- Effectiveness: Significant (most common)

**Accept:** Tolerate the risk with no action
- When: Cost of mitigation exceeds benefit
- Example: Low-impact operational risks
- Cost: Absorbed loss if risk occurs
- Effectiveness: Minimal (risk remains)

**Transfer:** Shift risk to third party
- When: Economical alternative exists
- Example: Insurance, outsourcing
- Cost: Transfer/insurance premiums
- Effectiveness: High (if effective transfer)

### 6.2 Mitigation Planning
For mitigation approach, develop:
- **Owner:** Responsible executive
- **Timeline:** Implementation schedule
- **Resources:** Required investment
- **Controls:** Specific mitigating actions
- **Metric:** Success/effectiveness measure
- **Status:** Current progress tracking
- **Contingency:** Backup if primary fails

### 6.3 Control Testing
Controls verified through:
- **Design Testing:** Are controls well-designed?
- **Operating Effectiveness:** Do controls work as designed?
- **Frequency:** Annually (critical), periodically (others)
- **Documentation:** Evidence of control operation
- **Remediation:** Corrective action for failures

## 7. RISK MONITORING & REPORTING

### 7.1 Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)
Selected for each major risk:

| Risk | KRI | Target | Frequency |
|------|-----|--------|-----------|
| **System Downtime** | System availability % | > 99.5% | Daily |
| **Data Breach** | Security incidents | 0 | Weekly |
| **Key Person Loss** | Turnover of division heads | < 10% annually | Monthly |
| **Regulatory Compliance** | Violations/findings | 0 major | Quarterly |
| **Customer Concentration** | Top 10 customer % | < 25% | Quarterly |
| **Liquidity** | Cash reserves (months) | > 3 months | Monthly |

### 7.2 Risk Register
Central repository maintained with:
- Risk ID and description
- Category and owner
- Likelihood and impact (current)
- Risk rating and trend
- Root causes
- Mitigation strategy
- Control responsibilities
- KRIs and thresholds
- Status and updates
- Next review date

### 7.3 Reporting Cadence

**Monthly:** Risk Committee
- New risks identified
- KRI updates and exceptions
- Control test results
- Mitigation plan progress
- Emerging risks
- Escalation items

**Quarterly:** Board of Directors
- Executive summary of top risks
- Key metrics and trends
- Significant changes
- Risk appetite assessment
- Strategic risk alignment

**Annual:** Executive Team & Board
- Comprehensive risk review
- Risk assessment updates
- Emerging risks for coming year
- Framework effectiveness assessment
- Training and awareness

## 8. EMERGING RISKS MONITORING

### 8.1 Emerging Risk Categories
Monitor for:
- Technological disruption
- Regulatory changes or proposals
- Geopolitical events
- Economic shifts
- Climate and ESG concerns
- Industry consolidation
- Pandemic/health threats
- Supply chain disruptions

### 8.2 Monitoring Approach
- Monthly emerging risk scanning
- Quarterly emerging risk assessment
- Industry publication review
- Peer and competitor analysis
- Regulatory guidance monitoring
- Expert interviews/advisory boards
- Scenario planning exercises
- Annual emerging risk workshop

### 8.3 Escalation
Emerging risks escalated when:
- Probability assessment reaches threshold
- Updated to risk register
- Added to Risk Committee agenda
- Board notification if significant
- Mitigation planning initiated
