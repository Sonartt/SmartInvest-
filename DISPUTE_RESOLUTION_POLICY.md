## 1. POLICY OVERVIEW

This Dispute Resolution & Complaint Handling Policy establishes procedures for addressing user complaints, disputes, and grievances fairly and efficiently.

### 1.1 Policy Objectives
- Resolve disputes quickly and fairly
- Maintain transparent communication
- Protect user rights and interests
- Prevent escalation to legal proceedings
- Improve service based on complaints
- Ensure compliance with regulations

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- Service quality complaints
- Transaction disputes
- Billing/payment issues
- Account access problems
- Data/privacy concerns
- Technical issues
- Terms and conditions disputes
- Conduct disputes

## 3. COMPLAINT HANDLING PROCESS

### 3.1 Complaint Triage & Classification

**Step 1: Initial Review (24-48 hours)**
- Log complaint and assign reference number
- Classify complaint type
- Assess urgency/severity
- Request additional information if needed

**Step 2: Intake Investigation (24-72 hours)**
- Review supporting documentation
- Locate relevant transaction records
- Interview involved personnel
- Preliminary assessment completed

**Step 3: Formal Investigation (5-10 business days)**
- Detailed analysis of facts
- Review of relevant policies
- Determination of liability
- Assessment of damages

**Step 4: Resolution & Communication (10-15 business days)**
- Develop resolution options
- Communicate findings to user
- Implement agreed resolution
- Close complaint file

**Step 5: Follow-up (30 days after resolution)**
- Verify resolution satisfaction
- Document lessons learned
- Update processes if needed
- Archive complaint file

### 3.2 Complaint Categories

| Category | Examples | Standard Timeline |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Billing/Payment** | Overcharge, duplicate charge, refund | 15 business days |
| **Service Quality** | Slow service, feature failure, error | 20 business days |
| **Technical Issues** | Account access, transaction failure | 10 business days |
| **Data/Privacy** | Unauthorized access, data misuse | 30 business days |
| **Conduct** | Harassment, discrimination, abuse | 25 business days |
| **Contract Dispute** | Terms disagreement, policy question | 20 business days |

### 3.3 Response Timeline

| Complaint Type | Acknowledgment | Investigation | Resolution |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Critical/Urgent** | 24 hours | 5 business days | 10 business days |
| **High Priority** | 48 hours | 10 business days | 15 business days |
| **Standard** | 72 hours | 15 business days | 20 business days |
| **Complex** | 5 business days | 20 business days | 30 business days |

## 4. INVESTIGATION PROCEDURES

### 4.1 Information Gathering
Investigators will:
- Request documentation from complainant
- Review transaction records and logs
- Access relevant account information
- Interview involved personnel
- Examine system records and backups
- Obtain third-party information (if needed)

### 4.2 Evidence Evaluation
- Assess credibility of evidence
- Verify authenticity of documents
- Cross-reference multiple sources
- Determine reliability and relevance
- Apply preponderance of evidence standard

### 4.3 Regulatory Consultation
For significant disputes:
- May consult with external legal advisor
- Coordinate with regulatory bodies (CBK, FRC)
- Request guidance on applicable regulations
- Ensure compliance with legal standards

### 4.4 Confidentiality During Investigation
- Investigative details kept confidential
- Information shared only with authorized personnel
- No public disclosure during investigation
- Final findings shared with complainant
- Third parties notified only as required

## 5. COMPLAINT OUTCOMES & REMEDIES

### 5.1 Possible Outcomes

| Outcome | Definition | When Applied |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| **Sustained** | Complaint found to be valid | Clear evidence supports claim |
| **Partially Sustained** | Some but not all aspects valid | Partial evidence or partial fault |
| **Not Sustained** | Complaint found to be invalid | Evidence does not support claim |
| **Unresolvable** | Cannot determine validity conclusively | Conflicting evidence or unclear facts |

### 5.2 Available Remedies

**Financial Remedies:**
- Refund of overcharged fees (full or partial)
- Service credit toward future fees
- Compensation for documented losses
- Reimbursement for third-party costs

**Service Remedies:**
- Service restoration
- Account correction or repair
- Feature access restoration
- Priority support upgrade (temporary)

**Procedural Remedies:**
- Policy change or clarification
- Process improvement implementation
- Training for staff members
- Communication to prevent recurrence

**Combination Remedies:**
- Multiple remedies applied together
- Tailored to specific complaint circumstances

### 5.3 Remedy Assessment Framework
Remedies determined based on:
- Severity of harm caused
- Duration of impact
- Complainant's loss or inconvenience
- Company's liability percentage
- Policy violations (if any)
- Precedent considerations
- Fairness and reasonableness

### 5.4 Maximum Remedy Limits

| Remedy Type | Limit |
|-------------|-------|
| **Financial Refund** | Full disputed amount + 20% damages |
| **Service Credit** | 3 months of service fees maximum |
| **Compensation** | Actual documented losses |
| **Priority Support** | 6 months maximum |

## 6. APPEALS PROCESS

### 6.1 Appeal Eligibility
Users may appeal if:
- Dissatisfied with investigation findings
- New evidence becomes available
- Investigation process was flawed
- Resolution was not implemented
- Time limit: 30 days from resolution notice

### 6.2 Appeal Procedure

**Step 1: Appeal Submission**
- Submit appeal within 30 days
- Include reference number
- Explain disagreement with outcome
- Provide new evidence (if applicable)
- Desired resolution stated clearly

**Step 2: Appeal Review**
- Independent reviewer assigned (not original investigator)
- Complete file review conducted
- New evidence evaluated
- Additional investigation if needed (5-10 business days)

**Step 3: Appeal Decision**
- Decision communicated within 20 business days
- Decision final (no further appeals available)
- Exception: If regulatory body requests review

### 6.3 Appeal Rights
- Users have right to one formal appeal
- May request escalation if appeal outcome unsatisfactory
- Escalation reviewed by executive management
- Final decision by Chief Complaints Officer

## 7. ESCALATION PROCEDURES

### 7.1 Automatic Escalation Triggers
Complaint automatically escalated if:
- Not resolved within standard timeline (+ 5 days grace)
- Investigation stalled or incomplete
- User requests escalation explicitly
- Legal action threatened
- Media attention/public complaint
- Regulatory inquiry received
- Pattern of similar complaints emerges

### 7.2 Escalation Levels

| Level | Responsible Party | Authority | Timeline |
|-------|-------------------|-----------|----------|
| **Level 1** | Complaint Officer | Resolve using discretion | 15 days |
| **Level 2** | Department Manager | Review and override decision | 10 days |
| **Level 3** | Director of Operations | Strategic override authority | 10 days |
| **Level 4** | Chief Executive Officer | Final authority (except regulatory) | 5 days |

### 7.3 Escalation Communication
- Complainant notified of escalation immediately
- New timeline provided
- Senior reviewer assigned within 2 business days
- Regular status updates provided
- Executive review completed

## 8. COMPLIANCE & REGULATION

### 8.1 Regulatory Coordination
For disputes involving regulatory matter:
- CBK (Central Bank of Kenya) consultation
- FRC (Financial Reporting Centre) notification
- Kenya Revenue Authority coordination (if tax involved)
- Consumer Protection Advisory notification
- Legal compliance verification

### 8.2 Regulatory Reporting
SmartInvest reports to regulators:
- Material disputes (> KES 1,000,000)
- Systemic issues (affecting multiple users)
- Conduct violations
- Data/security breaches
- On schedule: Quarterly reports

### 8.3 Regulatory Deadlines
- CBK dispute submission: Within 60 days
- FRC notification: Within 48 hours (breaches)
- Complainant notification: Required by regulation
- Resolution timeline: Varies by regulator

## 9. FRAUD & CRIMINAL COMPLAINTS

### 9.1 Fraud Complaint Procedure
If complaint involves suspected fraud:
- Preserved as evidence
- Forwarded to Investigations Team
- May be reported to law enforcement
- User kept informed of process
- Confidentiality maintained

### 9.2 Criminal Referrals
SmartInvest reports to authorities if:
- Evidence of crime committed
- Fraud > KES 100,000
- Organized crime suspected
- Identity theft involved
- Hacking/unauthorized access
- Money laundering indicators

### 9.3 Cooperation with Authorities
- Provide evidence to law enforcement
- Cooperate with investigations
- Preserve data as directed
- Testify if required
- User may be contacted by authorities

## 11. SPECIAL COMPLAINT CATEGORIES

### 11.1 Discrimination Complaints
Complaints alleging discrimination based on:
- Race, color, or ethnicity
- Gender, gender identity, or sexual orientation
- Religion or beliefs
- Disability status
- Age
- National origin
- Other protected characteristics

**Special Handling:**
- Accelerated investigation (10 business days)
- Senior management involvement
- Zero-tolerance enforcement
- Potential account termination
- Legal referral if appropriate

### 11.2 Harassment Complaints
Complaints involving:
- Unwanted contact or communication
- Threatening behavior
- Intimidation or coercion
- Sexual harassment
- Cyberbullying or abuse

**Special Handling:**
- Immediate suspension of harasser's account
- Investigation within 5 business days
- Protective measures for complainant
- Evidence preservation
- Law enforcement referral possible

### 11.3 Data/Privacy Complaints
Complaints involving:
- Unauthorized data access
- Privacy violations
- Data breach consequences
- GDPR/POPIA violations
- Cookie/tracking issues

**Special Handling:**
- Privacy Officer involvement
- Data Protection Authority notification
- Compliance review
- Forensic investigation
- Breach notification (if applicable)

### 12.1 Complaint File Contents
Each complaint file includes:
- Original complaint submission
- Reference number and timeline
- Investigation notes
- Evidence and documentation
- Interview summaries
- Decision and rationale

## 13. USER RIGHTS & RESPONSIBILITIES

### 13.1 User Rights
- File complaint without retaliation
- Fair and impartial investigation
- Timely resolution attempt
- Appeal opportunity
- Receive decision in writing
- Privacy and confidentiality
- Access investigation file (redacted)

### 13.2 User Responsibilities
- Provide accurate information
- Cooperate with investigation
- Provide evidence promptly
- Respond to requests for information
- Act in good faith (no frivolous complaints)
- Maintain confidentiality of process
- Not interfere with investigation

### 13.3 Prohibited Conduct
Users may not:
- File false or frivolous complaints
- Threaten or intimidate investigators
- Fail to cooperate without cause
- Publicly disclose investigation details
- Interfere with investigation process
- Attempt to bribe or pressure investigators

**Consequences:** Account restrictions, termination, or legal action

## 14. EXTERNAL COMPLAINT OPTIONS

### 14.1 Financial Ombudsman
Users may escalate to Financial Ombudsman if:
- Not satisfied with SmartInvest resolution
- Complaint exceeds KES 5,000,000
- Legal representation required

**Contact:** Kenya Financial Ombudsman  
**Website:** https://www.ombudsman.go.ke  
**Email:** info@ombudsman.go.ke

### 14.2 Consumer Protection Agency
Consumer complaints may be filed with:
- Kenya Bureau of Standards
- Consumer Federation of Kenya
- Local Consumer Protection Office
- Attorney General's Office (if fraud)

### 14.3 Regulatory Bodies
Financial disputes may be escalated to:
- **Central Bank of Kenya:** Banking/payment issues
- **Financial Reporting Centre:** AML/CFT concerns
- **Communications Authority:** Electronic commerce disputes
- **Data Protection Commissioner:** Privacy violations

### 14.4 Legal Action
Users retain right to:
- File civil lawsuit
- Seek damages in court
- Pursue criminal charges (if applicable)
- Engage legal representation
- Pursue class action (if applicable)
