## 1. GOVERNANCE OVERVIEW

This Corporate Governance Policy establishes SmartInvest's commitment to good governance, ethical business conduct, accountability, and transparency in the management and oversight of the company.

### 1.1 Purpose
- Establish clear authority and accountability
- Ensure ethical leadership and conduct
- Protect shareholder and stakeholder interests
- Ensure effective strategic direction
- Maintain regulatory compliance
- Build public trust and confidence
- Support sustainable value creation

### 1.2 Governance Principles
- **Accountability:** Clear responsibility for decisions and actions
- **Transparency:** Open and honest communication
- **Responsibility:** Obligation to serve stakeholder interests
- **Fairness:** Equitable treatment of all stakeholders
- **Integrity:** Ethical conduct and adherence to values
- **Competence:** Qualified leadership and decision-makers

### 1.3 Applicable Standards
- Kenya Companies Act
- Capital Markets Authority (CMA) guidelines
- Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) requirements (if regulated)
- Corporate Governance Code of Kenya
- International corporate governance best practices

## 2. BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### 2.1 Role & Responsibilities
The Board of Directors:
- Provides strategic direction and oversight
- Approves major policies and strategies
- Ensures regulatory and legal compliance
- Monitors executive management performance
- Approves annual plans and budgets
- Reviews and approves financial statements
- Manages risk and internal controls
- Oversees compensation and talent management
- Represents shareholder and stakeholder interests
- Conducts regular self-evaluation

### 2.2 Board Composition

**Size:**
- Minimum 5 directors
- Maximum 9 directors
- Mixture of skills and experience
- Balance of internal and external members

**Director Categories:**
- **Independent Directors:** Majority of the Board (minimum 50%)
- **Executive Directors:** CEO and potentially CFO (maximum 2)
- **Non-Executive Directors:** Recruited for expertise and perspective

**Independence Criteria:**
Director is independent if no material relationship with company:
- Not employed (currently or recently)
- No significant customers/supplier relationships
- No major shareholder status
- No cross-directorships or conflicts
- No consulting/professional relationships
- 3-year cooling-off from company operations

### 2.3 Board Diversity
SmartInvest targets:
- **Gender Diversity:** Minimum 30% representation
- **Ethnic Diversity:** Reflects stakeholder base
- **Age Diversity:** Mix of experienced and emerging talent
- **Skills Diversity:** Finance, operations, tech, legal, governance
- **Industry Experience:** Varied backgrounds and expertise

### 2.4 Director Qualifications
Directors should possess:
- Financial literacy and business acumen
- Professional qualification or equivalent experience
- Relevant industry knowledge or transferable skills
- Strategic thinking and decision-making ability
- Integrity and ethical commitment
- Availability for Board meetings and responsibilities
- No conflicts of interest with company

### 2.5 Board Tenure & Terms
- **Term:** 3-year term (subject to election/reelection)
- **Maximum tenure:** 12 years (two consecutive 3-year terms)
- **Rotation:** Staggered to ensure continuity
- **Election:** Annual shareholder vote
- **Removal:** May be removed by shareholder vote
- **Resignation:** Requires written notice and reasoned statement

## 3. BOARD COMMITTEES

### 3.1 Audit Committee
**Composition:**
- Minimum 3 members (all independent directors)
- Chair: Independent non-executive director
- Financial expertise required
- Reports to: Full Board

**Responsibilities:**
- Oversee financial reporting accuracy
- Monitor internal control system
- Evaluate audit function effectiveness
- Review related-party transactions
- Oversee compliance with regulations
- Monitor fraud and misconduct
- Recommend external auditor appointment

**Meetings:** Quarterly minimum

### 3.2 Risk Committee
**Composition:**
- Minimum 3 members (majority independent)
- Chair: Independent director (preferred)
- Diverse expertise encouraged
- Reports to: Board

**Responsibilities:**
- Oversee enterprise risk management
- Monitor risk appetite and tolerance
- Review key risk indicators
- Assess control effectiveness
- Monitor compliance with risk policies
- Oversee business continuity planning
- Monitor emerging risks

**Meetings:** Monthly minimum

### 3.3 Compensation Committee
**Composition:**
- Minimum 3 members (all independent)
- Chair: Independent director
- HR/organizational expertise
- Reports to: Board

**Responsibilities:**
- Review executive compensation strategy
- Approve CEO compensation
- Approve senior management compensation
- Monitor compensation competitiveness
- Oversee succession planning
- Review compensation policies
- Approve incentive plans and performance metrics

**Meetings:** Semi-annual minimum

### 3.4 Governance & Nominations Committee
**Composition:**
- Minimum 3 members (all independent)
- Chair: Independent director
- Governance expertise preferred
- Reports to: Board

**Responsibilities:**
- Identify and vet director candidates
- Recommend board appointments
- Manage director conflicts of interest
- Oversee board self-evaluation
- Monitor governance framework
- Review board policies
- Recommend director compensation

**Meetings:** Quarterly minimum

### 3.5 Committee Charters
Each committee operates under formal charter defining:
- Composition and independence requirements
- Powers and authority
- Key responsibilities
- Meeting frequency and procedures
- Reporting to Board
- Self-evaluation process

## 4. BOARD OPERATIONS & PROCEDURES

### 4.1 Board Meetings
- **Frequency:** Quarterly minimum (usually 5-6 annually)
- **Notice:** Minimum 7 days advance notice
- **Agenda:** Prepared in advance with supporting materials
- **Quorum:** Majority of directors
- **Minutes:** Detailed minutes maintained
- **Confidentiality:** Board discussions held in confidence
- **Participation:** In-person preferred, virtual acceptable

### 4.2 Board Decision-Making
- **Authority:** Board makes major decisions by majority vote
- **Executive Session:** Independent directors meet regularly without management
- **Delegation:** Authority delegated to committees and executive management
- **Documentation:** Decisions documented in board minutes
- **Escalation:** Material matters escalated to full Board

### 4.3 Board Materials
Directors receive:
- Advance agenda (7 days prior)
- Complete supporting materials
- Financial statements and analysis
- Risk reports and KRIs
- Committee updates and minutes
- Strategic and operational updates
- Compliance and regulatory updates

### 4.4 Induction & Development
New directors receive:
- Company orientation and briefing
- Key policy and procedure review
- Financial and operational overview
- Site visits and stakeholder meetings
- Mentoring from experienced director
- Board procedures training

**Ongoing Development:**
- Annual training and updates
- Industry and governance education
- Regulatory and compliance updates
- Strategic seminars and discussions

## 5. MANAGEMENT STRUCTURE

### 5.1 Executive Management
**Chief Executive Officer (CEO):**
- Responsible for overall company performance
- Implements Board strategy and policies
- Day-to-day operational management
- Prepares strategic recommendations
- Reports to Board and shareholders
- Approves within delegation authority
- May be Board member (executive director)

**Chief Financial Officer (CFO):**
- Manages financial and reporting functions
- Prepares financial statements
- Manages budgeting and forecasting
- Oversees accounting and controls
- Reports to CEO and Audit Committee
- May be Board member or reporting executive

**Chief Technology Officer (CTO):**
- Manages technology strategy and operations
- Oversees systems and infrastructure
- Reports to CEO
- Works with Risk Committee on cybersecurity

**Chief Risk Officer (CRO):**
- Manages enterprise risk framework
- Coordinates risk assessments
- Reports to Risk Committee and CEO
- Independent authority on risk matters

**Chief Compliance Officer:**
- Manages regulatory compliance
- Oversees policies and procedures
- Receives ethics reports
- Reports to Board Audit Committee and CEO

### 5.2 Executive Authority
Authority delegated to executives includes:
- **CEO:** Authority to commit company within Board-approved limits
- **CFO:** Financial management and budgeting authority
- **CTO:** Technology and infrastructure decisions
- **Department Heads:** Operational decisions within delegation

**Financial Approval Matrix:**
- < KES 100,000: Department manager
- KES 100,000-1M: Department head/executive
- KES 1-10M: CFO and CEO
- > KES 10M: Board approval required
- Strategic/non-budgeted: Board approval required

## 6. SHAREHOLDER RIGHTS & ENGAGEMENT

### 6.1 Shareholder Communication
Company ensures:
- Annual shareholder meeting (within 4 months of year-end)
- Quarterly or annual financial statements
- Annual report with governance disclosures
- Notice of special corporate actions
- Access to governance information
- Website with key documents
- Timely responses to shareholder inquiries

### 6.2 Shareholder Meetings
- **Annual Meeting:** Held for financial reporting and elections
- **Special Meetings:** Called as needed for extraordinary matters
- **Voting:** Majority of shares represented (in-person or proxy)
- **Minutes:** Prepared and retained
- **Transparency:** Open discussion on company matters
- **Q&A:** Shareholder questions addressed
- **Remote Participation:** Virtual attendance option provided

### 6.3 Voting & Proxies
- **One share, one vote:** Equal voting rights per share
- **Proxy voting:** Shareholders may vote via proxy
- **Independent tally:** Votes counted by independent party
- **Results disclosed:** Voting results posted/announced
- **Record:** Voting records maintained

## 7. CONFLICT OF INTEREST MANAGEMENT

### 7.1 Director Conflicts
Directors must disclose:
- Financial interests in matters before Board
- Related-party transactions
- Competing business interests
- Family or personal relationships
- Outside affiliations

### 7.2 Management of Conflicts
When conflict exists:
- Director discloses conflict
- Director absent from discussion/vote
- Matter evaluated on independent merits
- Transaction approved only if beneficial to company
- Documented in Board minutes
- Disclosed to shareholders (if material)
- Independent director review of transaction

### 7.3 Related-Party Transactions
All related-party transactions:
- Disclosed to Board and Audit Committee
- Evaluated for fairness to company
- Approved only if in company's interest
- Disclosed to shareholders
- Disclosed in financial statements
- Conducted at arm's-length terms
- No preferential treatment

## 8. INTERNAL CONTROLS & COMPLIANCE

### 8.1 Internal Control Framework
Board ensures:
- Designed control system
- Regular assessment of controls
- Material weaknesses addressed
- Testing and monitoring
- Documentation and evidence
- Audit function effectiveness
- Continuous improvement

### 8.2 Compliance Program
Company maintains:
- Written compliance policies
- Code of Conduct and ethics policies
- Compliance training program
- Compliance monitoring and testing
- Regular compliance reporting to Board
- Disciplinary procedures for violations
- Whistleblower protection

### 8.3 Risk Management
Board oversees:
- Risk identification and assessment
- Risk appetite and tolerance
- Key risk indicators and monitoring
- Risk mitigation strategies
- Crisis management procedures
- Business continuity planning

## 9. EXECUTIVE COMPENSATION

### 9.1 Compensation Philosophy
Executive compensation designed to:
- Attract and retain top talent
- Align with shareholder interests
- Reward performance and behaviors
- Competitive with market rates
- Transparent and disclosed
- Approved by Compensation Committee
- Approved by Board and shareholders (where required)

### 9.2 Compensation Components
- **Base Salary:** Fixed annual compensation
- **Performance Bonus:** Variable based on metrics
- **Long-term Incentives:** Stock options or grants (vesting over time)
- **Benefits:** Health, retirement, perquisites
- **Severance:** Terms defined in employment agreements

### 9.3 Performance Metrics
Executive compensation tied to:
- Financial performance (revenue, profitability, shareholder returns)
- Strategic objectives
- Operational performance
- Customer satisfaction
- Employee engagement
- Risk management effectiveness
- Compliance and ethics

## 11. ANNUAL BOARD EVALUATION

### 11.1 Self-Evaluation
Board conducts annual self-evaluation:
- Board effectiveness and dynamics
- Committee performance and composition
- Director performance and contribution
- Governance policies and procedures
- Access to information and resources
- Training and development needs
- Identified improvements and actions

### 11.2 Evaluation Process
- Anonymous surveys where appropriate
- Individual director discussions
- Committee self-evaluations
- Overall Board assessment
- Results discussed in executive session
- Action plan developed
- Progress tracked

### 11.3 Director Evaluation
Individual director evaluation considers:
- Attendance and participation
- Contribution to discussions
- Independence and objectivity
- Adherence to governance standards
- Committee performance (if applicable)
- Overall effectiveness

## 12. BOARD COMPENSATION

### 12.1 Director Fees
Board members may receive:
- Annual retainer fee
- Committee membership fee
- Committee chair additional fee

### 12.2 Approval Process

- Approved by shareholders
- Competitive with market benchmarks
- Disclosed in annual report
- Annually reviewed for appropriateness
