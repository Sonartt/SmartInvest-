## 1. POLICY OVERVIEW & SCOPE

This Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption Policy establishes SmartInvest's commitment to conducting business with integrity and in compliance with anti-corruption laws including the UK Bribery Act 2010, Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA), and Kenya's anti-corruption regulations.

### 1.1 Applicability
This policy applies to:
- All employees worldwide
- Board members and executives
- Contractors and consultants
- Joint venture partners
- Agents and representatives
- Third-party service providers
- All company subsidiaries and operations

### 1.2 Policy Principles
- Zero tolerance for bribery and corruption
- Compliance with international anti-corruption laws
- Fair competition based on merit
- Transparent and ethical business practices
- Reporting and investigation of violations
- Disciplinary action for breaches

---

## 2. DEFINITIONS

### 2.1 Bribery
**Offering, promising, giving, or authorizing the transfer of value to:**
- Government officials (direct or indirect)
- Private sector personnel
- Employees of customers or suppliers
- Any person in position of influence
- With intent to obtain/retain unfair advantage or induce improper action

**Examples:**
- Cash payments to officials
- Expensive gifts to decision-makers
- Lavish entertainment for business purposes
- Donations to beneficiaries of official's choosing
- Loans or equity stakes to relatives
- Job offers to family members
- Travel or accommodation beyond normal business practice

### 2.2 Corruption
**Acting dishonestly or improperly to obtain personal or corporate benefit:**
- Kickback schemes
- Bid-rigging or tender fraud
- Embezzlement and theft
- Conflict of interest violations
- Extortion or blackmail
- Money laundering
- Fraudulent licensing or permits

### 2.3 Facilitation Payments
**Small payments to government officials to expedite routine services:**
- Processing permits or licenses (without affecting outcome)
- Scheduling inspections or meetings
- Releasing goods from customs
- Connecting utilities or services

**SmartInvest Position:** Generally prohibited except in genuine emergencies where legal alternatives unavailable. Must be documented and approved by Compliance.

---

## 3. PROHIBITED CONDUCT

### 3.1 Direct Bribery
Employees must NOT:
- Offer or give anything of value to obtain business advantage
- Promise payments contingent on business decisions
- Provide gifts intended to improperly influence
- Offer employment or contracts to obtain favors
- Authorize third parties to offer bribes
- Accept bribes from suppliers or customers

### 3.2 Indirect Bribery
SmartInvest must NOT:
- Use intermediaries to provide bribes
- Conceal bribe payments through third parties
- Use complex transactions to disguise corrupt payments
- Partner with vendors known to provide bribes
- Create shell companies for corrupt purposes
- Use consultants or agents to circumvent policy

### 3.3 Improper Payments
Employees must NOT:
- Provide loans to government officials or their relatives
- Guarantee or provide personal financial commitments
- Offer shares or investment opportunities with preferential terms
- Make donations to recipients chosen by officials
- Purchase goods/services at inflated prices
- Provide lavish entertainment beyond normal business practice

### 3.4 Conflicts of Interest
Employees must NOT:
- Direct contracts to entities they have interest in
- Participate in procurement decisions affecting their companies
- Hire or contract with relatives for personal benefit
- Accept payments from vendors for influence
- Use business relationships for personal gain
- Fail to disclose conflicts affecting decisions

## 4. GIFTS & ENTERTAINMENT STANDARDS

### 4.1 Permitted Gifts
Employees may give gifts that:
- Are minimal in value (< KES 2,000)
- Are not cash or cash equivalents
- Are company-branded promotional items
- Are not offered with expectation of favor
- Are disclosed if protocol requires
- Are appropriate to business relationship
- Comply with recipient's ethical rules

**Examples of Permitted Gifts:**
- Company merchandise (pens, USB drives, branded apparel)
- Industry-standard conference materials
- Small samples of company products
- Flowers or a modest basket of goods
- Holiday gift baskets (under KES 2,000)
- Books or business magazines

### 4.2 Prohibited Gifts
Employees may NOT give:
- Cash, checks, bonds, or monetary equivalents
- Jewelry, watches, or luxury items
- Designer fashion or accessories
- Electronics or high-value technology
- Alcoholic beverages (unless moderate at event)
- Travel vouchers or gift certificates
- Artwork or collectibles
- Anything with personal customization/engraving

### 4.3 Gifts to Government Officials
**Special Restrictions:**
- Generally must be FREE OR MINIMAL VALUE only
- Must never contain anything of practical value
- No alcohol, tobacco, or similar items
- No personal items (clothing, accessories)
- Company promotional materials only
- Must be documented and disclosed
- Recipient's employing agency policy must be considered

### 4.4 Entertainment Standards
Employees may provide:
- Business meals during normal working hours
- Reasonable and proportionate meals during negotiations
- Modest entertainment (theater, sports events
- Attendance at industry conferences or trade shows
- After-work drinks or casual dinners during business visits

**Standards for All Entertainment:**
- Must be directly connected to legitimate business purpose
- Must be proportionate (not lavish or excessive)
- Must not be frequent or unusual for recipient
- Alternatives (virtual meetings, daytime events) preferred
- Must not include unrelated family members
- Must be documented with business purpose

## 5. GOVERNMENT RELATIONS

### 5.1 Interactions with Officials
Employees must:
- Maintain professional, transparent relationships
- Document all government interactions
- Obtain approval for meetings involving substantial interests
- Provide accurate and truthful information
- Comply with public records requirements
- Disclose conflicts of interest
- Never offer anything improper

### 5.2 Regulatory Compliance
When dealing with agencies:
- Applications must contain truthful information
- Financial reports must be accurate and complete
- No concealment or misrepresentation
- Cooperate with inspections and audits
- Meet disclosed timelines and requirements
- Appeal through proper channels if denied
- Report improper requests from officials

### 5.3 Political Activities
Employees may:
- Vote and support candidates as private citizens
- Volunteer time for qualified campaigns
- Donate personal funds to political causes (where legal)
- Participate in public advocacy

Employees must NOT:
- Use company funds for political donations (unless company policy allows)
- Pressure colleagues to support candidates
- Conduct political activity during work time
- Use company resources for campaigns
- Divert client/supplier funds to political causes

## 6. THIRD-PARTY MANAGEMENT

### 6.1 Vetting Requirements
Before hiring agents, consultants, or representatives:
- Conduct background check
- Assess reputation and track record
- Verify business legitimacy
- Review any compliance violations
- Request written certifications
- Request references from prior clients
- Screen against corruption watchlists

### 6.2 Contracting
Agreements with representatives must:
- Clearly define permitted activities
- Include anti-corruption representations and warranties
- Include compliance obligations and standards
- Specify compensation tied to legitimate services
- Prohibit facilitation payments without approval
- Include audit and inspection rights
- Include termination rights for violations
- Require anti-corruption certifications

### 6.3 Ongoing Monitoring
For all third parties:
- Review periodic contractor reports
- Verify business expenses align with services provided
- Monitor for unusual transactions or red flags
- Conduct periodic compliance validation
- Audit sampling of invoices and documentation
- Investigate suspicious activities promptly

### 6.4 Red Flags
Investigate if third parties:
- Request cash payments or unusual payment methods
- Lack clear business address or operations
- Have unclear relationship to decision-makers
- Request payment to off-shore accounts
- Have unexplained assets or lifestyle
- Pressure for expedited decisions
- Avoid documentation of activities
- Have prior corruption or fraud allegations

## 7. FINANCIAL & ACCOUNTING CONTROLS

### 7.1 Accuracy Requirements
All financial records must:
- Accurately reflect actual transactions
- Include business purpose documentation
- Match supporting invoices and receipts
- Be recorded in appropriate accounts
- Follow all accounting principles
- Maintain audit trail
- Remain available for review

### 7.2 Prohibited Practices
Employees must NOT:
- Create false invoices or receipts
- Establish off-book accounts
- Maintain hidden cash reserves
- Fail to record transactions
- Misclassify expenses
- Create shell companies for improper purposes
- Use multiple payment systems to obscure source/use
- Destroy records

### 7.3 Approvals & Authorization
Expenses must:
- Be pre-approved by appropriate authority
- Have documented business purpose
- Include supporting documentation
- Follow delegation of authority
- Be verified before payment
- Be segregated for high-risk transactions

## 8. INVESTIGATION & REMEDIATION

### 8.1 Reporting Violations
Employees must report suspected violations:
- To direct supervisor or HR
- To Compliance Officer directly: compliance@smartinvest.africa
- To Ethics Hotline (confidential): ethics@smartinvest.africa
- To external authorities if internal channels fail

**Protection:** No retaliation for good-faith reports

### 8.2 Investigation Process
Upon report of potential violation:
1. **Receipt & Assessment** (1-2 days)
   - Acknowledge receipt
   - Assess severity and urgency
   - Preserve evidence
   - Notify senior management

2. **Investigation** (5-20 business days typical)
   - Gather documentary evidence
   - Interview relevant parties
   - Obtain legal advice if necessary
   - Document findings

3. **Determination** (3-5 business days)
   - Analyze evidence
   - Determine if violation occurred
   - Assess severity and intent
   - Recommend action

4. **Resolution** (1-5 business days)
   - Implement corrective action
   - Discipline as appropriate
   - Remediate improper advantage
   - Communicate outcome

### 8.3 Disciplinary Action
Violations may result in:
- Written warning (minor cases)
- Suspension (moderate cases)
- Termination (severe cases)
- Recovery of improper gains
- Criminal referral (if applicable)
- Civil action for damages
- Barring from future contracts

## 10. RECORDS & DOCUMENTATION

### 10.1 Record Retention
SmartInvest maintains:
- Financial records for 7+ years
- Contract and agreement files
- Risk assessment documentation
- Training records (3+ years minimum)
- Investigation files
- Disciplinary records

### 10.2 Confidentiality

## 11. POLICY VIOLATIONS & CONSEQUENCES

### 11.1 Breach Response
Any breach results in:
- Immediate investigation
- Suspension of violator from duties (if severe)
- Remediation of improper advantage
- Assessment of business impact
- External notification (if legally required)
- Potential regulatory reporting

### 11.2 Scale of Discipline

| Violation Type | Severity | Typical Response |
|---|---|---|
| **Undisclosed minor gift** | Low | Training, counseling |
| **One lavish gift without approval** | Medium | Written warning, repayment |
| **Pattern of improper entertainment** | High | Suspension, possible termination |
| **Direct bribe offer** | Critical | Immediate termination, criminal referral |
| **Using intermediaries for bribery** | Critical | Termination, litigation |
| **Fraudulent financial records** | Critical | Termination, prosecution |
