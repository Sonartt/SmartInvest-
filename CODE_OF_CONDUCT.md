## 1. INTRODUCTION & PURPOSE

This Code of Conduct establishes ethical and professional standards for all SmartInvest employees, contractors, consultants, board members, and third-party partners.

### 1.1 Applicability
This Code applies to:
- All full-time and part-time employees
- Contractors and temporary workers
- Board members and executives
- Consultants and advisors
- Third-party vendors and partners
- All locations and operations

### 1.2 Commitment
SmartInvest commits to fostering a workplace that:
- Upholds integrity and ethical behavior
- Respects diversity and inclusion
- Protects health, safety, and wellbeing
- Complies with all applicable laws
- Promotes professional development
- Values accountability and transparency

---

## 2. CORE VALUES & PRINCIPLES

### 2.1 Integrity
- Conduct business with honesty and fairness
- Honor all commitments and promises
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Disclose material information appropriately
- Maintain confidentiality of sensitive data
- No deception or misrepresentation

### 2.2 Respect & Dignity
- Treat all individuals with respect
- Zero tolerance for discrimination or harassment
- Protect fundamental human rights
- Value diverse perspectives and backgrounds
- Foster inclusive and welcoming environment
- Support equal opportunity for all

### 2.3 Accountability
- Take responsibility for actions and decisions
- Admit and correct mistakes promptly
- Comply with policies and regulations
- Report violations appropriately
- Support colleagues in meeting standards
- Lead by example

### 2.4 Excellence
- Maintain high professional standards
- Continuously improve skills and knowledge
- Deliver quality work and service
- Pursue best practices
- Innovate responsibly
- Support team success

---

## 3. PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT

### 3.1 Workplace Behavior
Employees must:
- Maintain professional demeanor at all times
- Communicate respectfully and clearly
- Respect personal boundaries
- Arrive on time and complete assigned work
- Minimize personal use of company resources
- Dress appropriately for work environment
- Maintain clean and safe workspace

### 3.2 Conflict Resolution
When conflicts arise:
- Document the situation and relevant details
- Communicate directly with involved party first
- Seek resolution in good faith
- Involve management if necessary
- Follow formal grievance procedures if needed
- Maintain confidentiality throughout process
- Accept resolution professionally

### 3.3 Performance Standards
Employees must:
- Meet agreed-upon performance objectives
- Contribute effectively to team goals
- Seek feedback and coaching
- Document achievements and challenges
- Communicate regularly with manager
- Support performance improvement plans
- Celebrate team successes

## 4. ETHICS & COMPLIANCE

### 4.1 Legal & Regulatory Compliance
Employees must:
- Comply with all applicable laws and regulations
- Understand requirements relevant to their role
- Report compliance concerns to management
- Cooperate with audits and inspections
- Maintain accurate records
- Follow established compliance procedures
- Never encourage or tolerate violations

### 4.2 Conflicts of Interest
Employees must disclose:
- Financial interests in competitors
- Family or close relationships affecting work
- Outside employment or business interests
- Gifts or favors that might influence decisions
- Positions held in external organizations
- Any circumstance creating actual or apparent conflict

**Permitted Activities:**
- Secondary employment that doesn't compete
- Volunteer work and community service
- Personal investments in public companies
- Non-competitive outside interests

**Prohibited Activities:**
- Working for competitors during employment
- Soliciting customers or employees for outside benefit
- Using company information for personal gain
- Making decisions that benefit personal interests

### 4.3 Gifts & Entertainment
Employees may:
- Accept gifts of minimal value (< KES 2,000)
- Attend business meals and industry events
- Accept promotional items (branded goods)
- Participate in client entertainment

Employees may NOT:
- Accept cash gifts or equivalents
- Accept jewelry, electronics, or luxury items
- Accept travel or accommodation without prior approval
- Offer gifts expecting favorable business treatment
- Accept gifts from vendors during contract negotiations

**Disclosure Requirement:** Gifts > KES 2,000 must be reported to HR/Compliance

### 4.4 Confidentiality & Trade Secrets
Employees must:
- Protect confidential information from unauthorized access
- Use proprietary information only for authorized purposes
- Encrypt sensitive data on personal devices
- Dispose of confidential documents properly
- Limit discussion of confidential matters
- Maintain secrecy even after employment ends
- Never sell or leak trade secrets

**Confidential Information Includes:**
- Proprietary algorithms and methodologies
- Client lists and contact information
- Financial data and projections
- Strategic plans and initiatives
- Product development roadmaps
- Security systems and procedures
- Unreleased products or features

### 4.5 Intellectual Property
Employees understand that:
- Work created during employment belongs to SmartInvest
- Copyright, patents, and trade secrets are company property
- No royalties owed for work-related IP
- Personal projects must be clearly separated
- Employees must assist in IP protection efforts
- Moral rights attributed to creators appropriately

## 5. DISCRIMINATION & HARASSMENT

### 5.1 Anti-Discrimination Policy
SmartInvest prohibits discrimination based on:
- Race, color, or ethnicity
- Gender, gender identity, or sexual orientation
- Religion or beliefs
- Age or disability
- National origin or immigration status
- Marital or family status
- Genetic information
- Military service status
- Any other protected characteristic

### 5.2 Harassment & Bullying
Prohibited conduct includes:
- Verbal harassment or insults
- Physical intimidation or aggression
- Sexual harassment or unwanted advances
- Discriminatory jokes or comments
- Cyberbullying or malicious rumors
- Threatening behavior
- Intimidation or coercion
- Exclusion or ostracism

### 5.3 Sexual Harassment
Specific prohibited behaviors:
- Unwanted sexual advances or propositions
- Sexually explicit language or materials
- Unwanted physical touching
- Sexual jokes or innuendos
- Pressure for sexual favors
- Retaliation against complaints
- Quid pro quo harassment (reward/punishment for sexual response)

### 5.4 Response & Support
Victims of harassment may:
- Report to supervisor or HR immediately
- Contact confidential ethics hotline
- File formal complaint with documentation
- Request investigation and remediation
- Expect non-retaliation protection
- Access counseling or support services
- Pursue external complaint channels

## 6. HEALTH, SAFETY & WELLBEING

### 6.1 Workplace Safety
Employees must:
- Follow all safety procedures and protocols
- Report hazards and unsafe conditions
- Use required safety equipment
- Participate in safety training
- Cooperate with safety inspections
- Report injuries and near-misses promptly
- Never remove or disable safety devices
- Support safety culture organization-wide

### 6.2 Drug & Alcohol Policy
SmartInvest prohibits:
- Being impaired while working
- Possessing illegal drugs or alcohol at work
- Operating equipment while impaired
- Arriving under influence of substances
- Using company resources to purchase drugs/alcohol
- Selling or distributing controlled substances

**Permitted Activities:**
- Moderate alcohol at authorized company events
- Prescribed medications as directed
- Legal substance use off-duty (not affecting performance)

**Consequences:** Violation may result in immediate termination

### 6.3 Mental Health & Wellbeing
SmartInvest supports:
- Employee assistance programs (EAP)
- Mental health counseling and therapy
- Flexible work arrangements for medical needs
- Stress management resources
- Peer support groups
- Wellness programs and activities
- Non-discrimination against mental health conditions

### 6.4 Workplace Violence Prevention
Employees must:
- Never engage in violent behavior or threats
- Report threats immediately to security/HR
- Cooperate with threat assessment
- Follow emergency procedures
- Support safe environment for all
- Not bring weapons to workplace
- Never use workplace as venue for violence

## 7. SOCIAL MEDIA & REPUTATION

### 7.1 Social Media Guidelines
When posting about SmartInvest or work:
- Maintain professional and respectful tone
- Identify yourself if discussing company
- Don't share confidential or proprietary information
- Clarify personal opinions vs. company positions
- Follow company social media policy
- Respect privacy of colleagues and clients
- Avoid discriminatory or offensive content

### 7.2 Company Reputation
Employees should:
- Represent company professionally
- Support company reputation and brand
- Address complaints constructively
- Defend company from false accusations
- Report reputation threats to management
- Use company communication channels officially

### 7.3 External Communications
Employees must:
- Not speak to media on company behalf without authorization
- Refer media inquiries to PR/Communications department
- Obtain approval for interviews or public speaking
- Avoid making commitments on company's behalf
- Request approval before publishing about work-related topics

---

## 8. FINANCIAL INTEGRITY

### 8.1 Fraud Prevention
Employees must NOT:
- Falsify records or documents
- Manipulate financial data
- Embezzle company funds or property
- Submit false expense reports
- Engage in bribery or kickbacks
- Participate in Ponzi or pyramid schemes
- Misrepresent company financials
- Conceal financial misconduct

### 8.2 Expense & Travel
Employees must:
- Submit accurate and timely expense reports
- Retain receipts for all expenses
- Report actual expenses (no padding)
- Use company funds appropriately
- Follow spending authorization limits
- Comply with travel policy
- Document business purpose of expenses
- Seek approval before incurring major costs

### 8.3 Vendor & Supplier Relations
Employees must:
- Select vendors based on merit (quality, price, service)
- Avoid favoritism based on relationships
- Disclose conflicts of interest
- Document all vendor selection criteria
- Never accept bribes or kickbacks
- Treat all vendors fairly and ethically
- Follow procurement policies

---

## 9. DATA PROTECTION & PRIVACY

### 9.1 Personal Data Handling
Employees must:
- Collect only necessary personal information
- Use data only for stated purposes
- Secure personal data appropriately
- Limit access to authorized personnel
- Respect individual privacy rights
- Honor data protection regulations (GDPR, Kenya DPA, etc.)
- Report breaches immediately

### 9.2 Customer Information
Employees must:
- Protect customer confidentiality
- Never disclose customer data unauthorizedly
- Use customer information only for authorized purposes
- Comply with customer privacy preferences
- Secure customer data against unauthorized access
- Report unauthorized access immediately

## 10. WHISTLEBLOWER PROTECTION

### 10.1 Right to Speak Up
Employees have the right to:
- Report violations without fear of retaliation
- Use confidential reporting channels
- Request anonymity when possible
- Participate in investigations
- Refuse to participate in wrongdoing
- Report to external authorities if necessary

### 10.2 Protected Reports
Reports about:
- Legal or regulatory violations
- Safety hazards
- Financial fraud or impropriety
- Environmental violations
- Ethical misconduct
- Discrimination or harassment
- Data protection breaches

### 10.3 Anti-Retaliation
SmartInvest prohibits:
- Threatening reported employees
- Demoting or reducing responsibilities
- Terminating without cause
- Reducing compensation or benefits
- Excluding from meetings or information
- Assigning unwanted transfers
- Creating hostile work environment
- Making false accusations against reporter

## 11. COMPLIANCE VIOLATIONS & DISCIPLINE

### 11.1 Investigation Process
When a violation is suspected:
1. Report to supervisor, HR, or ethics hotline
2. Investigation initiated (confidential, documented)
3. Facts gathered from all relevant parties
4. Investigation completed (14-30 days typically)
5. Findings and recommended action determined
6. Appropriate discipline applied
7. Appeals process available (if applicable)

### 11.2 Corrective Actions
Depending on violation severity:

**Warning/Counseling:**
- First minor violations
- Documentation in personnel file
- Training or education provided
- Opportunity to improve

**Suspension:**
- Serious violations
- 1-30 days without pay
- Condition for reinstatement
- Final warning status

**Termination:**
- Severe violations (fraud, violence, repeated offenses)
- Immediate dismissal possible
- Final paycheck provided per law
- Criminal referral if applicable
- Potential civil action

### 11.3 Appeals
Employees may appeal discipline by:
- Submitting written appeal within 10 days
- Stating reason for disagreement
- Providing supporting documentation
- Requesting independent review
- Getting decision within 15 business days
- External arbitration available (if applicable)
