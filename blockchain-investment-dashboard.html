Blockchain Structure

Understanding the fundamental structure of blockchain technology for investment purposes.

Blocks

Immutable records of transactions grouped chronologically. Each block contains transaction data, timestamp, and a cryptographic hash linking to the previous block.

Chain

Sequential linking of blocks through cryptographic hashing. The chain structure ensures data integrity and prevents unauthorized modifications to transaction history.

Consensus

Distributed agreement mechanisms (Proof of Work, Proof of Stake) that validate new blocks. Essential for security and decentralization.

Decentralization

No central authority controls the network. Thousands of nodes maintain copies of the ledger, ensuring transparency and resilience.

    Risk Management & Hedging Strategies

    Comprehensive risk mitigation approaches for complex portfolios.

    Value at Risk (VaR)

    Estimate maximum loss at given confidence level (95%, 99%) over specific period.

    Example: 95% VaR of $10M at 1-day = Portfolio could lose $500K on worst 1 day in 20
    Expected Shortfall (CVaR)

    Average loss when VaR threshold is exceeded. Measures tail risk more accurately.

    Example: 95% CVaR = Average loss when exceeding VaR threshold
    Correlation Matrix

    Derivatives Trading & Strategies

    Advanced hedging and speculative strategies using options, futures, and other derivatives.

    Derivative Instruments
    Call Options
    Right to buy an asset at predetermined price. Profit from price appreciation.
    High Risk
    • Limited downside loss
    • Unlimited upside potential
    • Time decay risk
    Put Options
    Right to sell an asset at predetermined price. Profit from price decline.
    High Risk
    • Portfolio protection
    • Downside profit potential
    • Premium cost
    Bull Call Spread
    Buy call + sell higher strike call. Defined risk and reward.
    Medium Risk
    • Lower net cost
    • Limited profit
    • Defined max loss
    Iron Condor
    Sell call spread + sell put spread. Income from range-bound market.
    High Risk
    • Premium income strategy
    • Limited breakeven range
    • Maximum defined loss
    Index Futures
    Leverage exposure to market indices. High leverage, daily settlement.
    High Risk
    • 10-20x leverage
    • Margin requirements
    • Unlimited loss potential
    Currency Swaps
    Exchange currency cash flows. Manage forex exposure and interest rates.
    Medium Risk
    • Reduces forex risk
    • Lowers borrowing costs
    • Counterparty risk
    Greeks & Risk Metrics
    Delta (Œî)
    Rate of change in option price relative to underlying asset price (-1 to +1)
    Gamma (Œì)
    Rate of change of delta. Higher near-the-money options
    Theta (Œò)
    Time decay. Loss in value as expiration approaches
    Vega (ŒΩ)
    Sensitivity to volatility changes. Important for longer-dated options
    Rho (œÅ)
    Sensitivity to interest rate changes. Mostly for long-dated options
    IV (Implied Vol)
    Market's expectation of future volatility derived from option prices
